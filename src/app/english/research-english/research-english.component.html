Low-Impedance Human-Robot Interface

This was my Masters Degree project. I designed and built a robotic arm on which I installed a custom-built 6 degrees-of-freedom displacement sensor. The user interacts directly with the robot by pushing the sensor around which in turns makes the robot move. This approach is similar to what we can find in most cobots but adds a layer of safety and makes the whole system more responsive.

This is the first article published for this project. It was published at ICRA 2019. It describes the control scheme and the mechanism used to move the robot. This article was published in the ASME Journal of Mechanism and Robotics. It focuses on the mechanical design of the sensor and briefly touches on the control. This is my masters thesis which comprises both articles with some more material in between. The parts between the articles are in French while the articles are in their original English. This is a video of the final prototype of the robot and sensor. It shows the system working and how easy it is to move the robot with it.
